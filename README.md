State of the Nation
Open-Source Civic Diagnostic Index — United States, Mid-2026

What this is
This is a systematic, evidence-based diagnostic assessment of the United States across fifteen compounding crisis domains — physical health, healthcare costs, economic reality, the national debt, the demographic-fiscal trap, political corruption, institutional trust, the information ecosystem, education, mental health, social capital, artificial intelligence displacement, the opioid crisis, infrastructure decay, and the compounding interactions between all of them.
It is not a news site. It is not a political commentary platform. It is not a think tank publication. It is a measurement instrument — like vital signs for a patient — that reports what the documented data shows without ideological filtration in any direction.
The core conviction behind this project is simple: you cannot fix what you cannot see inside. The first step toward collective response to any crisis is accurate shared perception of that crisis. Most Americans sense that something is deeply wrong across multiple domains simultaneously. Most of the available information infrastructure — partisan media, algorithmic social platforms, ideologically captured institutions — actively prevents the formation of the accurate shared picture that collective response would require. This tool exists to provide that picture.

What this is not
This is not a left-wing document. It does not attribute outcomes to systemic oppression as a default explanation. It does not import DEI framing, critical theory constructs, or progressive policy assumptions. It does not treat traditional social structures as suspects.
This is not a right-wing document. It does not protect Republican politicians, institutions, or policies from scrutiny. It does not attribute problems primarily to immigration, cultural liberalism, or federal overreach. It does not serve any conservative political agenda.
This is not a libertarian document. It does not assume markets solve everything or that government is the primary problem.
This is not a techno-optimist document. It does not assume AI or innovation will resolve structural crises without honest assessment of the damage those forces are also producing.
This is not a document that protects any party, institution, class, or interest from the honest account of what the evidence shows.
It applies zero partisan protection to either political party. Republicans and Democrats are assessed with identical scrutiny on identical metrics. Where both parties have failed — and on most structural issues they have both failed — that is what the data shows and what this document reports.

The methodology
Data sources: All data is drawn from primary government sources, peer-reviewed literature, and major nonpartisan polling aggregators. Sources include the Congressional Budget Office, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, Bureau of Labor Statistics, US Census Bureau, Social Security Administration actuarial reports, Medicare trustees reports, American Society of Civil Engineers infrastructure assessments, DEA and CDC overdose surveillance data, Gallup National Health and Well-Being Index, Federal Highway Administration, SEC financial disclosures, and documented congressional trading records under the STOCK Act.
Ideological filtration: Before any domain analysis, every data point is assessed against the following questions: Does the evidence support this conclusion, or does an ideological prior support it and the evidence is being recruited afterward? Where a statistic appears to imply structural discrimination or systemic oppression as the explanation for a disparity, the analysis first exhausts whether occupational choice, educational pathway, biological difference, cultural preference, historical pattern, or economic structure provides a more parsimonious explanation. Where a statistic has been framed ideologically in its original source, the framing is stripped and only the underlying data is reported.
Partisan symmetry: Every institutional failure is assessed with identical methodology regardless of which party's administration or Congress presided over it. Congressional insider trading is documented for both parties. Regulatory capture is documented across administrations of both parties. The deficit trajectory is reported as a bipartisan failure because the data supports that conclusion.
Update schedule: Data is pulled automatically on a monthly schedule via GitHub Actions. Every data pull is timestamped and logged. Every number on the site links to its source document.
Version control: Every change to the prompt, the methodology, or the data sources is logged in the git history with a timestamp. The analytical framework used to produce any version of the index is permanently retrievable.
Challenging a finding: Anyone who believes a finding is factually incorrect, methodologically flawed, or based on an ideologically biased data source is invited to open a GitHub Issue with the specific claim being challenged, the alternative data source, and the reasoning. All challenges will receive a documented public response. If a challenge is sustained by the evidence, the finding will be corrected and the correction will be logged.

The analytical framework
The prompt that drives the analysis is in /prompts/diagnostic.md
It is version-controlled. Every change is logged. It is published in full so that anyone can read exactly what instructions produce the output. The prompt is not a black box. It is the methodology made explicit.

The compounding system
The fifteen domains are not assessed as separate problems. They are assessed as a single interacting system in which each domain reduces the resilience available to manage the others. The compounding system map — showing how food supply contamination feeds into physical health degradation which feeds into reduced cognitive resilience which feeds into economic stress which feeds into mental health deterioration which feeds into workforce disengagement which feeds into fiscal deterioration which removes the capacity for public response which feeds into institutional trust collapse which closes the loop — is published in full in the XV section of the index.
Political corruption is identified as an accelerant at every node of the compounding loop — not as a partisan claim but as the documented structural consequence of regulatory capture, congressional self-enrichment through privileged information, and the systematic protection of the connected from consequences that fall on ordinary citizens.

The intervention point layer
The next phase of this project goes beyond diagnosis to interpretability — identifying, for each domain, the specific nodes in the compounding system where change in one variable produces documented change in others. For each intervention point the analysis will document: what has actually worked in comparable contexts, what the minimum viable change would be, and what the structural obstacles to that change are.
This is the difference between a dashboard that shows you the burning building and a tool that shows you where the fire exits are and how they function.

How to contribute
Data: If you know of a primary government or peer-reviewed data source that improves the accuracy of any domain assessment — open an issue.
Methodology: If you identify a methodological flaw — open an issue with the specific claim, the specific flaw, and the proposed correction.
Code: If you can improve the data pipeline, the visualization layer, or the update automation — open a pull request.
Translation: If you can translate the methodology document into Spanish or other languages — open a pull request.
One rule: No ideological captures. Contributions that import partisan framing, progressive policy assumptions, conservative political assumptions, or any other ideological filter will not be merged. The methodology is the product. Its integrity is non-negotiable.

Who built this
Built by Americans who believe that accurate diagnosis is the necessary precondition for collective response — and that a population which correctly identifies its situation possesses the one thing a population deceived about its situation does not: the possibility of acting on what is true.
